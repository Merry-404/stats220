Gaming data visulisation

The Nature of the Data: The data used in this visual story is structured as rectangular (tidy) data, collected persistently via a Google Form. In this dataset, each row represents a single observation (an individual respondent’s submission), and each column represents a distinct variable derived from the form’s questions, along with an automatically generated timestamp. It captures the video gaming habits of 21 participants of UoA students. The data includes categorical (character) variables, such as favorite games and devices, numerical variables, such as daily gaming hours, and open-text responses detailing their gaming motivations.

What am I analyzing: Through this visual data story, I am analyzing the relationship between gamers’ preferences and their time commitment. Specifically, I want to explore whether playing a wider variety of games leads to more time consumed, which specific games demand the most time, and whether simple reasons like finding a game “interesting” truly capture the complex motivations behind gaming.

Does playing more games mean spending more time?

A scatter plot showing the relationship between the number of games currently played and the daily hours spent gaming. Generally, more games correlate with more time spent. However, there is a clear outlier: someone who plays 10 games but only spends 0.5 hours a day.

Which favorite game consumes the most time?

A scatter plot comparing favorite games with daily time consumption, where the size of the points represents the number of respondents. ‘League of Legends’ and ‘Genshin Impact’ have the largest points, indicating they are the most popular choices, with playtime varying widely from 1 to 5 hours.

When was this gaming data logged?

A bar chart showing the count of logged dates. It reveals that all 21 observations were collected on a single day, May 12, 2026.
